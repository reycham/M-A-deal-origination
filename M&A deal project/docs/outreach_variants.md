# Outreach Templates — UK MSP Deal Origination

Principal-to-principal tone. Short. Discreet. No pitch language.
The goal is one response: "I'd be open to a conversation."

Replace `{placeholders}` before sending. Personalise with one specific detail
from the company profile (years built, niche, region).

---

## Variant A — Cold email, succession angle

**Subject:** {Company Name} — confidential enquiry

Hi {First Name},

I came across {Company Name} while researching IT managed service businesses in {Region}. Having built it for {X} years, I imagine you've had occasional conversations about what a succession or exit might look like.

I work with a buyer who's actively looking to acquire a business like yours — founder-run, long-standing client relationships, recurring contracts. No auction process, no public announcement. Just an exploratory conversation to understand whether there might be alignment.

Would you be open to a brief call in the next few weeks? Completely confidential, no obligation.

{Your Name}

---

## Variant B — Cold email, buyer-mandate angle

**Subject:** Acquisition enquiry — {Company Name}

Hi {First Name},

My name is {Your Name}. I'm sourcing acquisition targets on behalf of a buyer looking specifically for UK-based IT managed service businesses — your profile fits what they're looking for closely.

I know this kind of outreach can come out of the blue, so I'll keep it short: if you've ever thought about a partial or full exit — whether now or in the next few years — I'd welcome a 20-minute conversation. Nothing formal, just an introduction.

Happy to share more about the buyer if it's of any interest.

Best,
{Your Name}

---

## Variant C — LinkedIn connection note (300 chars)

Hi {First Name} — I help a buy-side client find UK MSP acquisitions. {Company Name} came up in my research; looks like a strong, long-standing operation. Would you be open to a brief, confidential conversation about a potential acquisition? No pressure — happy to share details if helpful.

---

## Variant D — Follow-up (send 5–7 days after no reply)

**Subject:** Re: {Company Name}

Hi {First Name},

Just following up on my note from last week. No pressure at all — I appreciate these messages can land at the wrong moment.

If the timing isn't right, or you'd prefer I not reach out again, just say the word. But if there's any curiosity, even at a very early stage, I'm happy to have a quiet conversation.

{Your Name}

---

## Sending notes

- Use Smartlead or Instantly for sequencing (Variant A → Variant D).
- LinkedIn touch (Variant C) within 2 days of the first email.
- Stop after the follow-up if no reply — deal origination is a long game.
- **Never mention a price or multiple in outreach.** That conversation happens on a call.
- BCC your client on A-tier sends so they're in the loop.
